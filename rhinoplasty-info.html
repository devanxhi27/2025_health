Rhinoplasty – Nose Surgery for Function and Aesthetics

Rhinoplasty, commonly referred to as a “nose job,” is a surgical procedure designed to reshape or reconstruct the nose. It is performed to improve breathing, correct structural deformities, or enhance facial harmony. Many individuals choose rhinoplasty to correct issues such as nasal asymmetry, a prominent hump, or a deviated septum that affects airflow.

"Rhinoplasty is not just about appearance—it’s about restoring balance, confidence, and proper breathing function."

Why People Choose Rhinoplasty

There are several reasons why patients decide to undergo rhinoplasty. Some seek it for cosmetic improvement, while others require it for medical or reconstructive reasons:

  • To enhance facial symmetry and balance
  • To correct breathing difficulties caused by structural problems
  • To repair nasal injuries or birth defects
  • To restore confidence and self-esteem

Types of Rhinoplasty Procedures

Depending on the patient’s needs, different techniques can be used:

  • Open Rhinoplasty: A small incision is made at the base of the nose, allowing for precise reshaping of the nasal structure.
  • Closed Rhinoplasty: All incisions are made inside the nose, resulting in no visible scars and a quicker recovery.
  • Functional Rhinoplasty: Focused on correcting breathing problems and nasal function, often due to a deviated septum or injury.
  • Revision Rhinoplasty: A secondary surgery to improve or correct the results of a previous rhinoplasty.

Benefits of Rhinoplasty

Undergoing rhinoplasty can bring both functional and aesthetic improvements. Patients often experience enhanced breathing, better facial proportions, and increased confidence in their appearance. The goal is always a natural-looking result that complements each individual’s unique features.
